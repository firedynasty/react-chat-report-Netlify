## Dagger Concept

**Dagger** is a two-route combination that attacks the middle of the field:

### The Core Routes
1. **Deep Dig/Over** - 15-20 yards across the field
2. **Vertical Seam/Post** - Up the seam

These two routes create a **high-low stretch** on the middle linebacker or safety.

## How It Works

**The Conflict:**
- The seam holds the deep safety
- The dig comes in behind the linebackers
- Middle defender can't cover both

**Common Read:**
1. Peek at the seam (is safety jumping it?)
2. Main read is the dig behind it
3. Checkdown if both covered

## Visual Example
```
        FS
         |
    ----DIG----> (15-20 yards)
         |
         |
    SEAM/POST
         |
        MLB
```

- Everyone knows who gets the special route based on formation rules

## China Concept

**"China"** is a 3-level vertical stretch concept that attacks the deep, intermediate, and short zones on one side of the field.

## The Basic China Route Combo

**Three Routes (from outside in):**

1. **#1 (Outside)**: Corner or Deep Out
2. **#2 (Middle)**: Deep Dig/Over (18-20 yards)
3. **#3 (Inside)**: Shallow/Underneath (5-6 yards)

This creates a **diagonal stretch** down the field.

## Why It's Called "China"

Different theories:

- Some say it resembles the shape of China on a map
- Others say it's just a random code name
- Every team has different origins for their concept names

## How China Works

**The Read (High to Low):**

1. **Deep**: Corner route stretches the safety
2. **Intermediate**: Dig/Over in the void (money throw)
3. **Underneath**: Shallow for hot/checkdown

**The Conflict:**

- Forces defenders to cover all three levels
- Linebacker can't cover both shallow and dig
- Safety can't cover both corner and dig

## Variations

**"China Bow"**

- Add a post route from backside

**"China Drive"**

- \#2 runs a shallower dig (12-15 yards)

**"China Flood"**

- RB releases to flat for 4th option

## When to Use China

**Great against:**

- Cover 3 (attacks the levels)
- Cover 2 (dig splits the safeties)
- Works from any 3Ã—1 or trips formation

**The Key:** China is all about that **intermediate dig route** - it's usually the primary target, with the corner holding the safety and the shallow as the outlet. It's a rhythm passing concept where the QB knows exactly where each route will be.
